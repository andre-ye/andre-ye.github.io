### Gains from Trade
*Suppose that France and Germany both produce schnitzel and wine. France produces a bottle of wine at an opportunity cost of 2 pounds of schnitzel. Germany produces a bottle of wine at an opportunity cost of 3 pounds of schnitzel. Who has a comparative advantage in producing wine? In schnitzel?*

Because France has a lower opportunity cost to produce a bottle of wine, France has a comparative advantage in producing wine. On the other hand, Germany has a comparative advantage in producing schnitzel.

*Suppose France is currently producing 1 bottle of wine and 6 pounds of schnitzel, and Germany is currently producing 3 bottles of wine and 6 pounds of schnitzel. Demonstrate that France and Germany can both be better off if the specialize in producing only one good and then trade for the other. What are the gains from trade?*

If France specializes in wine, it will produce 4 bottles of wine. If Germany specializes in schnitzel, it will produce 15 pounds of schnitzel. France can trade 3 bottles of wine for 7 pounds of schnitzel. As a result, France has 1 bottle of wine and 7 pounds of schnitzel; Germany has 3 bottles of wine and 8 pounds of schnitzel. France gains 1 pound of schnitzel from trading; Germany gains 2 pounds of schnitzel from trading.

*At a price of $5, the quantity demanded in the market would be?*

At five dollars, the combined demand is $$9 + 3 + 51 = 63$$ pounds.

*If the price of loose-leaf tea rises from $3 to $4, the market quantity demanded would?*

The market quantity would decreased, according to the law of demand.

*Graph the equation* $$P = -8Q + 10$$ *. Is this the supply curve or demand curve?*

The equation has a negative slope, meaning it is a demand curve for which a greater quantity is demanded at a lower price.

*Rearrange the equation* $$Q = 5 –0.25P$$ *and sketch the graph. Is this a supply curve or a demand curve?*

The equation is the same; quantity demanded is higher when price is lower.

*Explain what happens to the demand curve for iPhones under the following conditions:*
- *Apple raises the price of iPhones.* Apple is changing the price of the iPhone; thus, this would cause a change in quantity demanded. While the demand curve stays put, there is a movement left along the demand curve.
- *Samsung raises the price of the Galaxy.* The Galaxy is a substitute for the iPhone. When Samsung raises the price of the Galaxy, demand for the Galaxy decreases and demand for the iPhone increases. Thus, this would shift the demand curve for the iPhone rightward.
- *The cost of iPhone apps goes up.* iPhone apps are a complementary good to the iPhone. Because the cost of iPhone apps increases, demand for the iPhone will decrease. Thus, this would shift the demand curve for the iPhone leftward.
- *New research shows that iPhones cause finger cancer*. This would shift the demand curve for the iPhone leftward.
- *A recession hits and incomes fall*. The iPhone is a normal good, not an inferior good. Thus, the demand curve for the iPhone would shift leftward as less people can afford to puchase iPhones.

*Consider the market for cars. Which determinant of demand is affected by each of the following events? Choose from: consumer preferences, prices of related goods, incomes, expectations, and the number of buyers.*
- *Environmentalists launch a successful One Family, One Car campaign*. Consumer preferences.
- *A baby boom occurred 16 years ago*. Number of buyers.
- *Layoffs increase as the economy sheds millions of jobs*. Incomes.
- *An oil shortage causes the price of gasoline to soar*. Prices of related goods.
- *The government offers tax rebates in return for the purchase of commuter rail tickets*. Prices of related goods.
- *The government announces a massive plan to bail out the auto industry and subsidize production costs*. Expectations.

### Market Equilibrum
*Until recently, world oil prices were quite high. Then, as alternative technologies and world-wide recession drove down the demand for oil, prices fell. ▪Saudi Arabia then relaxed its quantity controls and prices fell further. ▪As a result, investment in developing alternative oil sources, such as fracking, have fallen.” Eventually we expect this to have what effect on equilibrium price and quantity oil?*

Demand has been driven down, so the demand curve has shifted leftward. The supply curve for oil will stay the same, since there is not development into alternative oil sources. The equilibrium price and quantity hence both decrease.

*What will the final effect be on the market for Priuses?*

Oil is a complementary good with respect to Priuses. As a result, we can expect demand for Priuses to decrease.
