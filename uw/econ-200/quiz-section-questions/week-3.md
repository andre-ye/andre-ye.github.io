### Price Elasticity of Demand

#### Problem 1
*Draw the following:*
- *Perfectly elastic demand*. A horizontal line.
- *Perfectly inelastic demand*. A vertical line.
- *More elastic than $$P = -Q + P_0$$*. A line with a slope $$<\|Q\|$$ (smaller in magnitude).
- *Less elastic than $$P = -Q + P_0$$*. A line with a slope $$>\|Q\|$$ (larger in magnitude).

#### Problem 2
*Calculate the elasticity (using the midpoint formula) for movement from:*

*From $$(40, 2)$$ to $$(30, 5)$$ (in the form `(price, quantity)`)*. 
 
The percent change in quantity is

$$\frac{2-5}{3.5} \approx -0.8571$$

The percent change in price is

$$\frac{40-30}{35} \approx 0.2857$$

Dividing yields $$\frac{-0.8571}{0.2857} \approx -3$$. Therefore, the price elasticity of demand is $$-3$$. Demand is elastic.

*From $$(10,8)$$ to $$(7,20)$$ (in the form `(price, quantity)`)*. 

The percent change in quantity is $$\frac{8-7}{7.5}$$.

The percent change in price is

$$\frac{\left(10-20\right)}{15}$$

Dividing yields $$\frac{\left(10-20\right)}{15} = -0.2$$. Therefore, the price elasticity of demand when moving from the first point to the second point is $$0.2$$. Demand is inelastic.

#### Problem 3
*When the price of a bar of chocolate is $1.00, the quantity demanded is 
100,000 bars. When the price rises to $1.50, the quantity demanded falls to 
60,000 bars.*

*Calculate the price elasticity of demand using the mid-point method.*

$$\frac{\frac{100000-60000}{\frac{\left(100000+60000\right)}{2}}}{\frac{1-1.5}{\frac{\left(1+1.5\right)}{2}}} = -1.25$$

*Find the price effect.*

When the price rises to $1.50, there is an increased price width of $0.50. The quantity demanded is 60,000 bars. So, the price effect is:

$$\frac{60,000 \times 0.50}{2} = 15,000$$

*Find the quantity effect.*

When the price rises to $1.50, there is a decreased quantity width of 40,000 bars. The price was being sold at $1.00. So, the quantity effect is:

$$\frac{40,000 \times 1}{2} = 20,000$$

*What happens to total revenue?*

Total revenue decreases by $$-5,000$ dollars.

#### Problem 4
*If the price elasticity of demand for used cars priced between $3,000 and 
$5,000 is –1.2 (using the mid-point method), what will be the percent change in 
quantity demanded when the price of a used car falls from $5,000 to $3,000?*

1.2, since using the mid-point method changes in direction are equivalent to negating the elasticity.

#### Problem 5
*Which of the following has a more elastic demand in the short run?*

*Pomegranate juice or drinking water?*

Pomegranate juice, since it seems to be a luxury, whereas drinking water seems to be a necessity.

*Cereal or Rice Krispies?*

Cereal is a large scope of good; there are not many substitutes for cereal. However, there are many substitutes for Rice Krispies, since it is a smaller scope of good. Thus, Rice Krispies have more elastic demand in the short run.

*Speedboats or gourmet chocolate?*

Even though both products seem to be luxuries, gourmet chocolate occupies less of the buyer's income than speedboats. Thus, speedboats will have more elastic demand in the short run.

### Surplus
#### Problem 1
*The demand for football tickets at the UW is $$QD = 80,000 – 500P$$ and the 
supply is $$QS = 20,000$$.*

The equilibrium point occurs at their intersection, which can be solved for using the following process:

$$20,000 = 80,000 - 500P \implies 500P = 60,000 \implies P = 120$$

Thus, the equilibrium price is $120 and the equilibrium quantity is 20,000 tickets.

The consumer surplus is the area under the demand curve but above the equilibrium price. This is a range between $400 and $120 for price and a range from 0 to 20,000 tickets for quantity demanded. The surplus is thus $$(400-120)\times10,000 = 2,800,000$$$.

The producer surplus is the area above the supply curve but under the equilibrium price. This is a range between $120 and $0 for price and a range from 0 to 20,000 tickets for quantity supplied. The surplus is thus $$120 \times 10,000 = 1,200,000$$.

The total surplus is the sum of the consumer and producer surplus, yielding $$4,000,000$$.

#### Problem 2

$$QD = 46 - 2P$$
