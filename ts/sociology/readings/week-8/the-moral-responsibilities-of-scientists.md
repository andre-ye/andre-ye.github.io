### Introduction
- Question of moral responsibility evokes issues of competence, coercion, and causation.
- Moral responsibility of scientists is different.
  - Scientists are assumed to be capable moral agents and not under coercion.
- Moral responsibility of scientists hinged on issues specific to professional boundaries and the production of knowledge.
- Traditional view: scientists are not burdened with the same moral responsibilities as the rest of us.
- Two bases for moral responsibilities in modern life.
  - *General moral responsibilities*. We hold these as agents.
  - *Role moral responsibilities* expand upon our set of responsibilities, arising from taking on particular positions in society.
- Scientific freedom is argued to be essential. The knowledge that science brings is o valuable that other claims of social or moral responsibility can be relinquished.
- Social structures allowing for the reduction of scientific general moral responsibilities would eliminate the autonomy of scientists.
- Scientists must choose between shouldering responsibilities and giving up autonomy.

### Negligence, Recklessness, and Responsibility
- What does it mean when a person is morally responsible for some action?
  - Distinction between causal responsibility and moral responsibility.
    - *Moral responsibility* attribution of blame or praise.
- We are morally responsible for things we intend to bring about and to some extent side effects of our actions.
  - *Recklessness*: one is fully aware of the risks imposed onto others.
  - *Negligence*: one is not fully aware of the risks imposed onto others but should be.
- Moral agents must determine that risks are justified.
- How much foresight should we expect of an individual?
- Distinction between recklessness and negligence relies on the agent's thought processes.
- What level of foresight do we expect from scientists?
  - Standard of "reasonable person".
- *Should* scientists consider broader social implications?
  - Do role responsibilities trump general responsibilities to consider unintended consequences?

### The Role Responsibilities of Scientists
- If the goal of science is to develop knowledge about the world, then the role responsibilities of scientists should be structured around this goal.
- Scientists share important results, expect honest reporting of data, respond to valid criticism.
- Does this responsibility obliterate other responsibilities scientists have as human beings.

### General Responsibility vs Role Responsibility for Scientists
- It is argued that the pursuit of knowledge or truth is so important that nothing should hinder the pursuit.
- *Knowledge does not trump all other values*. There are finite prices we are not willing to pay for knowledge.
- Implications of science extend beyond the applications of scientific knowledge.
  - The impact of knowledge is far from controllable.
- Abandonment of general responsibilities in science is not warranted nor desirable.
- **When scientists relinquish their general responsibilities, they relinquish their autonomy.**
  - The general responsibilities must be considered, and are done so via review boards and standards rather than the scientists themselves.
  - Intensive oversight would devastate autonomy in science.
- The structure of science cannot shield scientists from their general responsibilities without also sacrificing their autonomy.
- No adequate social structure can take over scientific consideration of general responsibilities.
- Scientists are at the forefront of a dynamic field, and cannot relinquish general responsibilities.

### Conclusion: Difficult Weighings Ahead
- The search for truth is not transcendent, but it is good.
- Should pursuing some piece of knowledge be forbidden?
- Scientists do not need to give up their autonomy.
