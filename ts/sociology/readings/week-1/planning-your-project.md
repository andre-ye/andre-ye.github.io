## Notes on  "Planning Your Project - An Overview"
Andre Ye, 3/26/21

<br>

---

<br>

- **Firstly,** find a research question worth investigating that will lead to a research question worth investigating.
  1. Find a topic specific enough to let you master a reasonable amount of information on it in the time you have.
  2. Question that topic until you find questions that interest you.
  3. Deduce what evidence you need to support your answer.
  4. Determine whether you can find this evidence.
- You can't march through the next steps of *assembling the argument, planning, drafting, and revising* - it will be a back-and-forth, zigzag process.
- Much of writing will be note-taking, but one should also take not to write *reflectively* and to *understand*.
  - Do you disagree with a source?
  - Can you summarize the source or positions?
  - Do you have any random thoughts, problems, or hunches?
- **Write to encourage critical thinking and greater understanding.**
