## Notes on "Woman as Other" from *The Second Sex by Simone de Beauvoir
Andre Ye, 4/17/21

<br>

---

<br>

### Introduction
- de Beauvoir was born in Paris to a bourgeois family.
- de Beauvoir focused her energy on defining the authentic human life of her feminism.

<br>

---

<br>

### Content
- Humanity is male, and man defines woman relative to him.
- Woman is defined and differentiated concerning man, and not vice versa.
- He is the Subject, he is the Absolute, and she is the Other.
- In the most primitive society, one finds expression of duality between Self and the Other.
- Otherness is a fundamental category of human thought.
- No group can ever be the One without setting the Other.
- We find in consciousness a fundamental hostility towards every other consciousness - the subject can be posed only in being opposed.
- The other ego sets up a reciprocal claim; this reciprocity, however, has not been recognized between the sexes.
  - Why do women not dispute male sovereignty?
- Privilege often depends on the equality of numbers, but women are not a minority.
- Parallel between women and the proletariat; neither formed a minority of separate collective unit of mankind.
  - Yet, there have not always been women, but not proletarians.
- Otherness is absolute in the case of sex relations because it lacks the incidental nature of historical facts. The natural condition is beyond the possibility of change.
- Women do not say "We"; men say "women"; women say "women".
- Women lack the means to organize themselves; they have no past, history, or religion of their own.
  - They live dispersed among mails.
- Women are divided among different groups; the division of the sexes is a biological fact, not an event in human history.
