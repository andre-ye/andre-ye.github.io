## Notes on "Black Reconstruction and the Racial Wage" from *Black Reconstruction in America* by W.E.B. Du Bois
Andre Ye, 4/17/21

<br>

---

<br>

- Northern industries knew that universal suffrage could not stand gainst organized industry; it could not move the foundations of Northern industry's power: tariff, money, debt.
- Two alternatives:
  - Democracy should establish a dictatorship of the proletariat.
  - Oligopoly where a handful of masterful men organized capitalism under their control.
- Reconstruction was an economic revolution on a mighty scale, not simply a fight between master and former slave.
  - Many attempts to paint this era as an interlude of petty politics.
  - Rather, it was a series of efforts to learn a living in new and untried ways.
- Reconstruction was a labor movement of black men trying to find work, of white men disinherited of land and labor, of the turn of white Northern migration southward, of oligarchy and monopoly to restore anachronism.
- White laborers were convinced that the degradation of black labor was more fundamental than the uplift of white labor.

<br>

- Political success of racial separation, which overthrew Reconstruction.
- Laboring class unity relies on the assumption that laborers will unite in their opposition to capitalists.
- Race has been carefully planned and slowly evolves; white laborers that received a low monetary wage were compensated by psychological wage.
- Blacks were always being threatened by the substitution of white labor.
- These are so widespread that they hold back organized effort that is the only hope of survival.
- If white and black in the South were intelligent and free, there would be a friendship.
