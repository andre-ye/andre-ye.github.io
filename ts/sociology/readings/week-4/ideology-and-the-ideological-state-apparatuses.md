### Biography
- Louis Althusser (1918-1990) was born in Algiers.
- In WWII, Althusser was imprisoned in a Nazi concentration camp.
- Althusser joined the French Communist Party afterwards.
- Later, Althusser murdered his wife and was confined to a mental hospital.

### Introduction
- Not only take into account *state power* and the *state apparatus*, but *ideological state apparatuses* (ISAs).
- In Marxist theory, the SA contains the government, the army, the police, etc. that provide "functions by violence".
- ISAs: religious, educational, family, legal, political, trade-union, communications, cultural, etc.
- There is one SA, but there are multiple ISAs.
- SA belongs in the public domain, but ISAs lie in the private domain.

### Ideology is a Representation of the Imaginary Relationship
- Two theses on the structure and functioning of ideology.

#### The First Thesis
- Thesis 1: *ideology represents the imaginary relationship of individuals to their real conditions of existence.*
- Religious ideology, ethical ideology, etc.
- Ideology does not correspond to reality, but it makes an allusion to reality.
- Ideologies are interpreted to discover the reality behind their representation of the world.
men represent themselves in ideology not by their real conditions of existrence, but their relation condiitons of existence represented to them.*
- At the center of every ideological representation of the world is a relation containing the "cause".
- Thesis that the imaginary nature of the relation underlies all imaginary distortion we can observe.
- All ideology - imaginary distortion - represents the imaginary relaitonship of individuals to the relations of production and the relaitons we derive from them.
  - It does not represent the existing relations of production.
- Why is the representation given to individual relation to social relations an imaginary question?

#### The Second Thesis
- Thesis 2: *ideology has a material existence*.
- Ideas or representations that make up ideology do not have an ideal and spiritual existence of ideas.
- What extent ideas have emerged is defined by ideological appratuses.
- Ideology exists in material ideological apparatuses.

### Ideology Interpellates Individuals as Subjects
- **There is no ideology except by the subject and for subjects**.
- The category of the subject is constiutive of all ideology.
- The category of the subject is only constitutive of ideology insofar as ideology can constitute concrete individuals as subjects.
- Ideology is nothing but its functioning in the material forms of existence of functioning.
- We are subjects, and thus ideological subjects; we live "naturally" in ideology.
- We are always already subjects and practice the rituals of ideological recognition.
- We are given a conciousness by our incessant practice of ideological recognition, but we do not have any scientific knowledge of the mechanism of this recogition.
- All ideology hails or interpellates concrete individuals as concrete subjects.
