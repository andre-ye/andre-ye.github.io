### Introduction
- Thousands of white Americans have died from the virus, but African American deaths are disproportionate.
- African-Americans comprise 70% of the dead in Louisiana despite making up 33% of the population.
- African-Americans are more likely to have preexisting health conditions.
- Black people are poorer, more likely to be unemployed, and given inferior health care.
- Black vulnerability is heightened by federal ineptitude in coronavirus response.
- Higher-income neighborhoods have 6 times more testing than poorer neighborhoods.
- This exacerbates the impact of the coronavirus

<br>

<br>

### Race and Class
- Deaths are the predictable outcome of decades of disinvestment and institutional neglect.
- Race and class are not "more impeding to social mobility"; they cannot be separated from each other.
- Intersecting threats of hunger, eviction, and unemployment drive African Americans to infection.
- Poverty reinforces ideological assumptions about race; characteristics about poverty are conflated with race.
- Racism or racial assumptions about African Americans influence how they are cared for in the healthcare industry.

### Systemic Issues and Medical Care
- Black people do not simply face a problem of exclusion; the deeper problem is U.S. society itself.
- Life expectancy for the average white person has gone in reverse; a phenomenon driven by alcoholism, opioid abuse, and suicide.
- Unequal access to healthcare may be important in the context of a pandemic.
- The U.S. has the highest youth and infant mortality rates among wealthy countries.
- What are officials prepared to do about it?
- Taylor proposes the rapid expansion of Medicaid and Medicare.

### Transformational Change
- National crises force us to look at poverty and inequality.
- Expressions of concern sound good at press conferences.
- Cities large and small have committed to attracting corporations rather than public institutions.
- Public hospitals shave been closed, schools starved out of the investment, etc.
- It is possible for governments to act in ways that help people.
- Resolving flaws comes into conflict with assumptions of governance.
