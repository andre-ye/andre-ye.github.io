Agenda

Some of the ideas I have been mulling on. Please see PhD SOP for one aspect of my interests, and great articles I've been reading.

Rewards and Judgments on Qualitative AI Work

As AI ventures into domains like creative writing, journalism, and philosophy, I am struck by the sense that although we cannot entirely formalize the correctness of a piece of AI work, we still can judge AI work as "better" or "worse". In fact, much of the work of literary critics, philosophers, and essayists in general is to reflexively critique and develop an understanding of the normative form of the essay in their domain. What does this look like for AI?

Qualitative Knowledge Certification

One of the most powerful uses of LLMs is to apply existing and produce new qualitative knowledge. But what are the kinds of reasons we would want to believe in the knowledge these models produce?

Machine Concept Definition and Neologism Learning

The computational abstractions which have been useful for interpretability may be useful for defining concepts. Can we represent the concepts we yearn to capture by finding the right set of sparse autoencoder features, or latent vectors?

Socratic Topologies

Thinking through a concept is like mapping out space. Maybe what we need is not "better AI assistants" per se but a map format which is better conducive towards concept AI tools.

An Ontology for Everyone

LLMs are increasingly good at annotating data according to highly custom ontologies. Maybe one role for a human is in figuring out what kind of ontology is useful for them.

Parallel Human-AI Interactions

What if your AI interrupted you? What interactions with AI were not just turn-by-turn conversations but had multiple simultaneous tracks?
